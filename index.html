About

Vasiliki is a textile artist working primarily on the loom, where thread, tension and time become sculptural constructions. [Replace this with a short bio.]

Weaving is the language through which I explore the invisible connections that link people, nature, and time.

Using threads, metal, wood, and natural materials that already bear the traces of their own lives, I create works in which memory, decay, and transformation take on a tangible form.

I am interested in the moment when matter ceases to be merely an object and becomes a vessel of experience.

My work navigates between light and darkness, presence and absence, the personal and the collective. It does not seek to provide answers, but rather to create a space for encounter, where the viewer is invited to recognize the invisible bonds that connect us to one another and to the world.

Exhibitions & Press

1996 Internationale Handwerksmesse (IHM) – Munich, Germany Greek National Pavilion

My participation in the Internationale Handwerksmesse (IHM) in Munich was one of the first major milestones in my artistic career. At the historic Theresienhöhe exhibition grounds, in the Central National Pavilion of Greece, I presented works that combined the Greek weaving tradition with a contemporary artistic vision. The warm response from the public reaffirmed my belief that weaving can transcend cultural boundaries and serve as a universal language of communication.

1997 “The Weave of the Sky” Solo Exhibition of Textile Art · “Diavasi” Cultural Center – KETHEA

The solo exhibition “The Weft of the Sky” was a visual exploration of the human capacity to rebuild one’s life. Through the act of weaving, the thread was transformed into a symbol of transformation, hope, and personal freedom, engaging in a meaningful dialogue with the work of KETHEA.

1998 “Nima” Solo Exhibition · “Melina” Cultural Center – City of Athens

The exhibition “Thread” explored weaving as a contemporary visual language, combining woven works, painting on woven surfaces, and installations made of threads. Influenced by the Bauhaus philosophy, it highlighted the creative dialogue between matter, space, and human experience. The industrial character of the “Melina” Cultural Center, located in the old hat factory, served as an integral part of the visual experience.

2002 “Primordial Memory” Solo Exhibition of Textile Art · Kea Center for Contemporary Art

The exhibition “Primordial Memory” paid tribute to the indigenous cultures that discovered and developed the art of weaving as a fundamental form of human expression. Inspired by Goethe’s phrase “One beat, a thousand myriads of threads,” it explored thread as a common element running through different cultures and eras, highlighting weaving as a vehicle for memory, creation, and cultural continuity.

2004 “UNITY” Proposal for the Olympic Stadium (Athens 2004)

“UNITY” was a large-scale visual and architectural proposal for the area surrounding the Olympic Stadium (OAKA), in the context of the “Athens 2004” Olympic Games. The proposal was positively evaluated for its aesthetic approach but was not implemented due to high construction costs and complex technical requirements. The work explored the unity of peoples, the constant movement of populations, and the interaction of cultures, proposing a visual allegory for the deeper human condition: that beyond borders, races, and places of origin, human existence remains shared.

2015 Metrostages Syntagma Metro Station – STA.SY., Athens

As part of STA.SY.’s Metrostages program, I presented two visual art installations and woven works at the Syntagma Metro Station. The exhibition brought the art of weaving into the public space, creating an open dialogue with everyday commuters and highlighting art as part of the urban experience.

2019 8th International Biennial of Contemporary Textile Art (WTA) – Madrid, Spain Theme: Sustainable City · Work: “Metamorphosis”

My work “Metamorphosis” was selected to be presented at the 8th International Biennial of Contemporary Textile Art (WTA) in Madrid, alongside works by artists from around the world. Under the theme “Sustainable City,” the work explored metamorphosis as a process of personal and collective evolution, opening a dialogue about humanity’s relationship with matter, memory, and the environment. Weaving served as a common meeting ground where experience is transformed into form and meaning.

2019 “Captivity” Group Exhibition “Water and Freedom” · Byzantine & Christian Museum, Athens

As part of the group exhibition “Water and Freedom,” held at the Byzantine & Christian Museum, my installation “Captivity”—a composition of wire mesh and threads—was presented. Through the juxtaposition of rigid metal and fragile thread, the work explored the tension between confinement and the profound human need for freedom.

2019 Vasiliki Sofianou reintroduces the art of weaving, revealing the essence that lies in union Press · LiFO / The Good LiFO · 23 September 2019

A LiFO feature on Vasiliki Sofianou’s practice and philosophy of weaving—not as tradition alone, but as a way of life. The piece explores fiber art as a medium of union and presence, and her collaborations bringing weaving into contemporary fashion and everyday objects.
